Cheese Burger Recipe

A cheeseburger is a classic fast-food and diner dish made with a juicy, seasoned beef patty topped with melted cheese, usually served inside a soft bun. It often includes fresh toppings like lettuce, tomato, onions, and pickles, along with condiments such as ketchup, mustard, or mayonnaise.

Ingredients required:

  • Burger buns (1 per burger)
  • Ground beef (about 150–200 g per patty)
  • Cheese slices (cheddar is most common)
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Tomato slices
  • Onion slices (optional)
  • Pickles (optional)
  • Condiments: ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise (as preferred)
  • Salt and pepper (for seasoning the patty)
  • Oil or butter (for cooking the patty or toasting the buns)
  • Optional extras: bacon, mushrooms, avocado, or any other favorite toppings.

Method for making a cheeseburger:

  1. Prepare the patty

    1. Season the ground beef with salt and pepper.
    2. Shape it into a round patty slightly larger than the bun (it will shrink while cooking).

  2. Cook the patty

    1. Heat oil or butter in a pan over medium heat.
    2. Cook the patty 3–4 minutes per side for medium doneness (adjust time for rare/well done).
    3. In the last minute, place the cheese slice on top of the patty and cover the pan briefly to melt it..

  3. Toast the bun

    1. Cut the burger bun in half.
    2. Lightly toast the cut sides in a pan or oven until golden.

  4. Assemble the cheeseburger

    1. Spread condiments on the bottom bun.
    2. Add lettuce, tomato, and onion slices.
    3. Place the cheesy beef patty on top.
    4. Add pickles if using.
    5. Cover with the top bun.

  5. Serve

    1. Serve hot with fries, chips, or a side salad.
